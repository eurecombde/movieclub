Eurecom Movie Club

This is the homepage for the Eurecom Movie Club, a club for movie loving technologists studying at the French riviera.

Want to come watch a movie? We'd love to have you with us! The schedule is to show one movie every week, on Fridays around 17:30 if no other message is given. It's free to join, but we wont provide you with candy - bring your own! All movies are shown at the Eurecom Amphi.

Program

Program

Tucker and Dale vs Evil (09-05-2014)

The two hillbilly friends Tucker and Dale recently bought a cabin in the woods and are about to spend their vacation there. Their plan is foiled, however, by a group of college kids who are doing a camping trip in the same woods and assume the two friends to be serial killers. Confusion and a bloodbath ensues.

Amadeus (18-04-2014)

Nine years after the masterpiece «One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest» from 1975, director Miloš Forman is back. Telling the story of Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, Amadeus collected a nice 8 Oscars back in 1984. Join us for an exhibition on the genius, the music, and the insanity of one of the greatest classical composers the world has ever seen.

Office Space (11-04-2014)

Everyone that has been on the Internet for a while will inevitabley encouter memes sooner or later. A movie that has brough in it's faire share of those is the comedy «Office Space» from 1999.

Portraying Peter Gibbons, a disgruntled programmer at Initech, spends his days "staring at his desk" instead of actually working, is convinced by his girlfriend to attend an "occupational hypnotherapy" session during which the therapist dies of a heart attack, leaving Peter hypnotized.

The now completly relaxed protagonist discovers how easy life becomes when you just ignore the rules of the rat race that is most people's work life.

Life is Beautiful (La Vita è Bella) (04-04-2014)

Robert Benigni is quite something of a man. It can be hard to play in the movie you are directing, but Beningi is one of only two people who have directed themselves into recieving an Oscar for Best Actor. However, he's also in the rare company of people to have directed themselves into a Razzie for Worst Actor, sharing the honors with folks like Sylvester Stallone, Kevin Costner and Prince, to name a few.

Anyway, we're focusing on «Life is Beautiful» this friday, the movie that won him the Best Actor, along with Best Foreign Film and Best Music. In one of the highest rated movies of all time (8.6 on IMDb), Benigni will take us back to Italy in the 30s, to a Jewish family trying to survive Holocaust.

The Man from Earth (28-03-2014)

Professor John Oldman (David Lee Smith) is expected to become the next head of the anthropology department. Despite being loved by his students and appreciated by his colleagues, he nevertheless decides to quit his job and leave. During an unexpected farewell celebration at his home, his 10 year long friends can't help to ask him why. The movie then begins when he then starts asking, "What if…".

The movie was made for only 200 000$, but it's one of the best in its domain. Like Oldman, you'll be left wondering "what if", and the thought will stay with you long after the movie is done.

Master and Commander (21-03-2014)

The name Peter Weir might not be familiar to you, but I'm pretty sure you've heard of some of his movies. Does The Truman Show or Dead Poets Society ring a bell? Both of them (excellent!) movies he directed before he did «Master and Commander» in 2003.

The movie is based on a whopping 20-volume series of novels written by Patrick O'Brian, which although fiction, are based around the actual events that took place during the Napoleon wars. The movie is mostly inspired by the novel «The Far Side of the World», and follows Captain Jack Aubrey (Russel Crowe) and ship surgeon Stephen Maturin (Paul Bettany) on their adventures on the H.M.S Surprise as they are sent on a hunt for the French corsair Acheron.

«Master and Commander» took home 2 Oscars (nominated for 8 more!), and among them Best Sound Editing, so this will also be a splendid occasion to test the surround setup in Amphi!

Ferris Bueller's Day Off (14-03-2014)

We're kicking it off this season with the greatest high school movie from the 80s, say hello to Ferris Bueller everyone!

Today it's quite common to shy far away from any movie set in a high school environments, but there was a time when they managed to make quality content with this theme. This is the highlight from that period.
